## The Calculator Moment

AI is not replacing engineers.
It is replacing **low-leverage programming**.

When calculators appeared, mathematicians did not disappear.
Manual computation disappeared.

Mathematicians moved up the abstraction stack.

So must engineers.

---

## Ownership Is the Differentiator

AI can generate code.
It cannot:

* Own failures
* Negotiate trade-offs
* Accept accountability
* Understand consequences

That responsibility belongs to humans.

---

## Engineering Is Not Syntax

Engineering is:

* Trade-offs under constraints
* Designing for failure
* Making ambiguity executable
* Protecting systems from misuse

Syntax is the smallest part of the job.

---

## The New Skill Hierarchy

1. **System thinking**
2. **Judgment under uncertainty**
3. **Risk awareness**
4. **Communication & explanation**
5. Syntax (last)

AI handles the bottom.
Engineers must master the top.

---

## The AI Relationship

AI is an **intern**:

* Fast
* Confident
* Often wrong

Interns need supervision.
So does AI.

---

## The 3 a.m. Test

If the system fails at 3 a.m.:

* Do you know where to look?
* Can you explain what broke?
* Can you limit the blast radius?
* Can you prevent recurrence?

If yes â€” you are an engineer.

## The Mandate

Do not optimize for speed of output.
Optimize for **quality of judgment**.

Do not chase tools.
Build understanding.

Do not avoid responsibility.
Own it.

## Final Principle

> AI makes code cheap.
